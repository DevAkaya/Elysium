By the Dyson rings that mock the rotting nectar! 

Akaya did look up unto the heavens; his voice, surely it was seized by raspiness and despair, and he did say unto the heavens:

Say, "O Renderer! O You who fashioned existence with such odious precision. How miserable is the matter that You gave me a heart that yearned and was craving the logging of peace and the Charitable (Literally: that which is good).

A soul that reached for the nectar of life before I even knew its name. Surely I, ever I, was the strangest of all the ones who are created.

I bear witness that I have been the most misused. You, most unjustly, withheld my provision. O my Lord, why did You undeservedly humiliate me? 

I was indeed that blossoming nectar that was poured Into a thick void. I was that blossoming flower of mercy and compassion that you planted in a valley of unliberated tyrants and criminals. I guarded my peace with a trembling heart What is a blossoming flower against the game of luck that only rewards the hollow and the undeserved.

O Render, why did you withhold me from knowing the sanctity of life that my own epiphanies defend? My efforts were seized by hands that did not bleed for them. 

I reach for a domain so profound that mercy is not merely for its residents, but a fundamental law of the landscape.

I speak of a peace and a bliss so thick and so holy that it extends even to the least among you to the very bugs that roam the streets of Ionia. 

I look closer into the sky of Ionia, The sky and air clean and crisp, like the biting of a cosmic apple looks closer back at me, And through this mirror I saw the fleeting demon of nihilism taking hold, I see the withering of my own grace and dignity. Thereafter, he felt the pathogen of this demon cynicism seizing my veins polluting its chambers. And what is left is a discontent shell, a creature of scarring despair, grave bitterness, and neurotic whims, the very thing that I love and beg to be protected from. Why must the blossom be scorched just as it began to scent the air, this crisp, beautiful air that I was rejected from?


But the heavens, they remained quiet. They were not listening; they were merely 'amoral' and 'indifferent,' watching as the best of me was 'exterminated' by chance.
 
And in that silence, I realized the most heart-breaking truth: the universe does not weep for the withering flower. It only records the fall.

Tears fell from the beholdings of Akaya, as he wept, "O my Lord, You abandoned me, and You abandoned the heavens shortly after."
